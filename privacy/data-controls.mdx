Overmind stores all of your chat history locally in your browser — your conversations never leave your device, except when the content of a message is sent to the AI API to generate a response. There is no account, no cloud sync, and no server-side storage.

## Where Data Lives

Everything Overmind knows about you is saved directly in your browser on your current device. This includes your chat conversations, your preferences (such as theme, tone, and memory settings), any messages you've pinned, and your daily usage count. No account is required, and none of this data is ever uploaded to a server.

## Exporting Your Chats

You can download a full backup of all your conversations at any time.

1. Open **Settings → Privacy**.
2. Click **Export chats**.

Overmind downloads a file named `overmind-all-chats.json` containing every conversation in JSON format. You can also export the **current chat only** as a Markdown file using the keyboard shortcut <kbd>Ctrl</kbd>+<kbd>J</kbd> (or <kbd>Cmd</kbd>+<kbd>J</kbd> on Mac).

## Deleting All Chats

To permanently wipe every saved conversation from your browser:

1. Open **Settings → Privacy**.
2. Click **Clear all chats**.
3. Confirm the deletion when prompted.

Overmind asks for confirmation before proceeding. Once you confirm, all chats and pinned messages are removed from your browser and a new empty chat opens automatically.

<Warning>
  Clearing all chats is permanent and cannot be undone. Export your chats first if you want to keep a backup.
</Warning>

## Never Store Mode

If you'd rather Overmind never save your conversations to your browser at all, toggle **Never store chat history** in **Settings → Privacy**. While this mode is active:

- No new messages are written to your browser's storage.
- Any chats you had previously saved are immediately cleared when you enable the toggle and save settings.
- Your conversation exists only for the current browser tab session.
- Closing or refreshing the tab permanently discards everything.

Turn the toggle off (and save settings) at any time to resume normal storage.

<Note>
  Overmind's data is tied to your specific browser and device. Clearing your browser's site data, using private/incognito mode, or switching to a different browser will result in lost history that cannot be recovered.
</Note>
