The Behavior settings tab controls how Overmind formulates and delivers responses — from response depth and conversational tone to language preferences and factual caution. Dialing in these settings makes every conversation feel more tailored to your needs.

## Opening the Behavior Tab

Click **Settings** in the sidebar, then select the **Behavior** tab.

---

## Thinking Mode

The **Thinking style** dropdown determines how much processing Overmind applies before it responds. A hint below the dropdown updates dynamically to describe the selected mode.

| Option | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Fast answer** | Respond concisely and directly. Keep answers short. Lower temperature (`0.4`) for tight, predictable replies. |
| **Balanced** *(default)* | Respond clearly with a helpful amount of detail. A good all-purpose mode for most conversations. |
| **Step-by-step reasoning** | Reason carefully and explain the solution step by step. Best for complex problems, math, or multi-part questions. Higher temperature (`0.8`) for more exploratory answers. |

---

## Tone

The **Tone** dropdown sets the default conversational style for all of Overmind's responses. The [Response Enhancer plugin](/customization/plugins) actively injects this setting into the system prompt when enabled.

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Neutral** *(default)* | Balanced and even-handed — neither overly warm nor stiff. |
| **Friendly** | Warm and encouraging. Approachable and personable in every reply. |
| **Formal** | Professional and clear. Precise language, no slang or filler. |
| **Quirky** | Playful and slightly humorous. Adds personality and wit to responses. |
| **Concise** | Very direct and brief. Gets to the point without elaboration. |

---

## Language

The **Preferred language** dropdown tells Overmind which language to use by default. Overmind respects this preference in all replies unless you explicitly ask for a different language mid-conversation.

Available options include: English, Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Mandarin, Japanese, and Korean.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Mode

The **Anti-Hallucination Mode** dropdown instructs Overmind to be more cautious and transparent about uncertainty. When active, Overmind is more likely to say "I'm not sure" or "I don't know" rather than confidently guessing.

| Option | Behavior |
|--------|----------|
| **Off (Normal)** | Standard confidence — no additional caution applied. |
| **Moderate** *(default)* | Overmind flags uncertainty when it is not highly confident. Avoids fabricating details. |
| **Strict (Strongest)** | Maximum caution. Overmind will refuse to guess and explicitly says "I don't know" whenever it lacks certainty. |

<Tip>
  Enable **Strict** Anti-Hallucination Mode whenever you are doing factual research, verifying claims, or working with information where accuracy is critical. It significantly reduces the risk of plausible-sounding but incorrect answers.
</Tip>

## Identity

The **Identity** tab (a separate tab in Settings) lets you set a custom name that Overmind uses to refer to itself inside the conversation — for example, rename it to "Aria" or "Assistant" in the system prompt. The UI label at the top of the page always stays **Overmind** regardless of what you enter here; only the model's self-reference in dialogue changes.

---

## Language & Safety

The **Language** tab (also separate from Behavior) gives you two additional controls:

- **Swearing mode** — Choose whether Overmind never swears, always swears naturally, or only uses profanity when the context clearly calls for it (default: *Only when allowed*).
- **Safety level** — Set how conservatively Overmind handles risky or sensitive topics. Options are **Strict**, **Moderate** *(default)*, and **Permissive**.

---

## Saving Your Changes

Click **Save** at the bottom of the Settings modal to apply all Behavior changes. Settings are stored locally in your browser and persist across sessions.
